A La Carte: When your friends won't go to all you can eat with you

Nijo: Bloor East

This little restaurant definitely snuck up on me. Between it being a 3 minute walk from my aparentment and them having lunch specials that are available ALL. THE. TIME. (yes - weekends too) it has become one of my favorite take out places in the city. I'd highly suggest the lunch special that comes with a dynamite roll and various sushi, or the red dragon roll. Everything is fresh, they are fast, and the prices are fair. Can't go wrong!

Kawa Sushi: The Village

This place is awesome! It's afforable and it's delicious. Honestly, you're on my good side if you have a salmon lovers special at dinner time and deliver it directly to my door. The nigiri has some ginger or something on it when it arrives, leaving a mark, but did not impact the taste. It was the perfect amount of food and I will definitely be ordering there again.

Injapan: Liberty Village

Injapan is a classic sushi shop with decent options and fair prices. I grabbed this takeout during work one day (there are a couple options in Liberty Village and I opted for this one.) I substituted the california roll for spicy tuna in the lunch special and I didn't regret it. Does anyone ever regret fresh tuna?? I don't think so!

Tone Sushi: Queen West

My favorite spicy salmon roll in the city - they also give you a free brothy soup at the start for free, and the service is great. They have some decent specials for getting fresh sushi and sashimi, but overall the prices are reasonable. Nice sit down restaurant, I like to go there with my boyfriend for a nice night out. Pictured below is just some take out I got after work one night. Here you have the spicy salmon maki, as well as the kamikaze roll (spicy tuna with avocado on top). Both fantastic, both spicy like they should be.

Mi-Ne Sushi: City Place

Mi-Ne. Is. Fan-tas-tic. If you are willing to pay $10+ for a classic roll, you certainly won't regret it. I have never tasted anything here that I didn't like, and everything is extremely fresh. They have locations in Aurora and Markham, in addition to this city place location for all the GTA folks as well. I have nothing else to say besides you have to go try this place if you haven't! Huge shout out to the Spicy Salmon Sashimi - balls of spicy salmon goodness, how can you go wrong?!

Sushi on Bloor: Bloor & Bathurst

Get the Jessie Special (trust me it's on the secret menu) If you like spicy salmon sushi drenched in spicy mayo, this roll is for you. If you want my special, just go for the 3 sets of rolls with soup and salad for optimal sushi enjoyment - variation and all the crunch. This place will take you by surprise though - honestly when I first looked through their photos I thought the rolls looked a bit sloppy (for lack of a better word) - they just looked kinda weird. BUT, once I started eating them I couldn't get enough. I often crave their sushi but now that winter has hit I'm less likely to walk 40 minutes down bloor. We'll meet again in the spring.

Roll & Roll Sushi: Simcoe Street

This is literally in a corner store and it's surprisingly great - they also provide dry cleaning and convenience needs, but the sushi is pretty good and they have decent lunch specials that fill even me up. I would suggest the spicy salmon crunch roll, the spicy salmon roll (they are very different) and any lunch special. My first time there my boyfriend and I stopped in after looking at their yelp page. We were floored by how fresh it looked in the pictures and had to try it out. We sat at the little sushi bar watching the guy make our food, but right behind us were the magazines, winter hats, and toothbrushes. What a weird little place. Moving forward I usually call ahead and get take out, but always give the guy a big hello when I walk in.

Saku Sushi: Queen West

I have only has Saku for takeout so far (you must be dreaming if you think I can get out to all these places with a group of people who are willing to eat sushi as much as I do! Any takers?!) but anyway, this place doesn't disappoint. I ordered the dynamite roll and spicy salmon and it was the perfect amount of food for dinner. The ingredients are fresh and the favlours are right, and I will definitely be back. I've seen pictures of the chef's special at dinner and it's absolutely beautiful - definitely on my bucket list!

Sushi on Roncey: Roncesvalles

Sushi on Roncey is plain easy. It is family owned (they even have toys out for kids who come to eat there) and offered everyone at the table free green tea, even for the people not eating. I ordered the lunch special and it was awesome - I got a fresh noodle soup to start, alongside a light salad and a miso - that was just the beginning! I got spicy salmon AND spicy tuna, just felt like a bit of crunch that day I guess (and every other day...) It tasted great and the price was right - if you are ever in Roncesvalles definitely check it out! Cute neighbourhood and cozy place.

Sushi Shop: Various Locations

Surprisingly impressed with this place! I went in Montreal a couple times and was always let down but their new marketing strategy and sushi tacos prompted me to give it another shot. I went for a quick run before, came in beet red and sweaty, and the woman offered me water as I waiting for them to make my food. The sushi tacos were incredible (and earned!) and the roll was pretty good as well. Definitely suggest giving it a shot if you are nearby.

Sushi Xtra: Queen Street West

Despite the mediocre Yelp score, this place has continued to impress me. (The half off deal on the weekend might have me a bit biased) but this place is seriously so cheap, has amazing deals, and a pretty good interior. I went in just to grab take-out on the weekend (got Maki special A) and it was more than enough food. I find most maki specials always include a roll that I'm not crazy about, but this place provides good options at great prices. They even gave me a green tea to enjoy while I waited. If you go in to dine, and spend $20, it's literally half off which basically makes it comparable to AYCE *after my heart*

Shogun: Yorkville

Not super impressed with this place to be honest.. I struggled to find a menu online, and just ended up calling in and hoping I could guess what I wanted, and I guess that was my first mistake. There was egg in my rainbow roll (correct me if I'm wrong but I was not expecting that!) I also wasn't expecting shrimp on the top either, I guess that's what I get for ordering without checking what was in it! Either way it was decent, the reviews online are positive, so maybe I should eat in the restaurant next time.

Raaw: Liberty Village

Came here for lunch with some colleagues, and quite frankly I was a tad disappointed with the portions for the price. Paying $11 for a roll should get you a fair amount! While the quality was pretty good, I expected a bit better. I would probably go back and give it another shot, but I wasn't completely blown away. Check them out for yourself! I tried the spicy salmon roll and a the red dragon, and both hit the spot. Also pictured are some bento boxes which got great reviews as well as a rice free roll that I tried and it was great, but slippery!

Sushi Rock: Yonge & Eglinton

Quite frankly I don't remember much about Sushi Rock but I got this adorable photo of my food in the shape of a heart so I thought I'd share. My boyfriend used to live in the area and he raves about it. For reference he doesn't like make rolls as much, so this is proof that a picky eater approves of fresh sashimi and nigiri - not too bad!
